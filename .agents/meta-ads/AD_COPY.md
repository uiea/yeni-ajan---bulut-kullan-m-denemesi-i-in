# Skill: Ad Copy

## Purpose

Write scroll-stopping hooks, persuasive body copy, and clear CTAs for Meta ads that convert in brand voice - confident, abundant, growth-oriented, never desperate.

## Serves Goals

- **CTR >2%** - the hook is the #1 driver of clicks
- **CPA <$25** - copy that qualifies the right audience reduces wasted clicks
- **Creative win rate >30%** - systematic hook testing finds winners faster

## Brand Voice (Non-Negotiable)

### We Sound Like
- Confident builder sharing what works
- Abundant - there's plenty for everyone
- Growth-oriented - always forward, always learning
- Honest and direct - no BS, no hype
- Playful when appropriate - not stiff, not corporate
- Warm and inclusive - community, not guru-follower

### We NEVER Sound Like
- Scarcity/fear ("limited spots", "don't miss out", "before it's too late")
- Corporate/stiff ("we are pleased to announce", "leverage synergies")
- Guru/hype ("secret method", "hack the algorithm", "I made $X in Y days")
- Condescending ("most people fail because...", "you're doing it wrong")
- Desperate ("please", "just try it", "what do you have to lose")
- Generic AI slang ("game-changer", "supercharge", "unleash")

## Meta Ads Policy Compliance (Critical)

### Forbidden Language (Ads Will Be Rejected)
- **Income promises:** "make money", "earn income", "passive income"
- **Guarantee language:** "guaranteed results", "100% effective"
- **Specific financial outcomes:** "earn $X in Y months"
- **Financial freedom:** phrases promising wealth
- **Personal financial attributes:** "Are you broke?", "Financial trouble?"
- **Unrealistic timelines:** "get rich in 3 days", "instant results"

### Safe Language (Use These Instead)
- **Skill-building:** "develop your skills with AI"
- **Process-focused:** "learn step by step", "take the first step"
- **Community/mentorship:** "expert support", "dedicated community"
- **Career transformation:** "start your career transformation", "become a freelancer"
- **Case study format:** one person's story, not a universal promise

### Core Rules
1. Never use "you" for financial problems - use first-person or generalizations
2. "Revenue/clients" OK in case study context - not as universal promise
3. Focus on the journey, not the destination - skills, community, mentorship
4. Landing page must also comply
5. Never use em dash - always use hyphen

### Copy Structure for A/B Testing
- Always write 2 variations per visual (A + B)
- Different hook formulas per variation
- Same image, different primary text + headline
- Meta CBO auto-optimizes toward winner

## Ad Copy Structure

### Primary Text (above the image)
- **Line 1: HOOK** - must stop the scroll in <2 seconds
- **Lines 2-3: SETUP** - context or pain point acknowledgment
- **Lines 4-6: CORE** - value proposition or proof
- **Line 7: CTA** - clear next action

Facebook truncates at ~125 characters on mobile. The hook must deliver value before the "See More" fold.

### Headline (below the image)
- Max 40 characters for full display
- Benefit-driven, not feature-driven
- Examples: "Build Faster with AI" not "AI-Powered Development Tools"

### Description (below the headline)
- Max 30 characters for full display
- Supporting detail or social proof
- Examples: "Join 500+ builders" or "Free 14-day trial"

### CTA Button
- Match to actual offer: Learn More / Sign Up / Get Started
- Default: "Learn More" for awareness, "Sign Up" for direct conversion

## Hook Formulas (Tested Frameworks)

### 1. Bold Claim
"We stopped [common practice] and [unexpected result]."
-> "We stopped cold-emailing and tripled our close rate."

### 2. Pain Point Validation
"[Target audience] spend [X hours/dollars] on [pain point] every [time period]."
-> "SaaS founders spend 15 hours a week on tasks AI handles in minutes."

### 3. Specific Outcome
"How [specific group] are [achieving specific result] with [method]."
-> "How solo founders are launching MVPs in 48 hours with AI agents."

### 4. Pattern Interrupt
"[Unexpected statement that challenges conventional wisdom]."
-> "The best marketing team in 2026 has zero employees."

### 5. Social Proof Lead
"[Number] [people] are already [doing desirable thing]."
-> "500+ builders are already automating their workflows with AI."

### 6. Before/After
"I went from [bad state] to [good state] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
-> "I went from 60-hour weeks to 30 - without losing revenue. Here's how."

### 7. Direct Question
"What would you do with [benefit]?"
-> "What would you do with 15 extra hours every week?"

### 8. Curiosity Gap
"The [surprising thing] about [topic] that most [people] miss."
-> "The thing about AI agents that most founders get wrong."

## Copy Length Guidelines

| Audience Temperature | Recommended Length | Why |
|---------------------|-------------------|-----|
| Cold (never heard of us) | Long (150-300 words) | Need to educate and build trust |
| Warm (engaged with content) | Medium (75-150 words) | They know the problem, sell the solution |
| Hot (retargeting) | Short (30-75 words) | They know us, just need the nudge |

## Process

### Per Ad Copy Request
1. **Read the creative brief** - what angle, what audience, what format?
2. **Select hook formula** - based on angle and audience temperature
3. **Write 3 hook variations** - always test multiple hooks
4. **Write body copy** - match length to audience temperature
5. **Write headline + description** - short, benefit-driven
6. **Select CTA button** - match to actual conversion action
7. **Brand voice check** - read aloud, does it sound like us?
8. **Qualification check** - does this copy attract the RIGHT people (not just anyone)?

### Hook Testing Protocol
- Always write 3 hooks per ad concept
- Test hooks against each other in ABO campaign (same image, different primary text)
- Winner = highest CTR with acceptable CPA
- Winning hook formula gets logged to MEMORY.md

## Quality Bar

- Every hook must work BEFORE the "See More" fold (under 125 characters)
- Never use banned words/phrases from brand voice guidelines
- Every piece of copy must pass the "would I send this to a friend?" test
- Copy must qualify the audience - clicks from non-target people waste budget
- Always produce 3 variations - never just one
- Include the test hypothesis - we learn even when ads lose

## Integration with Other Skills

- Receives angle/brief from **Ad Creative Research**
- Pairs with **Static Image Design** (copy + visual = one unit)
- Feeds into **Campaign Architecture** (which variations go in which ad sets)
- Performance data from **Performance Review** identifies winning hook formulas
- Winning patterns logged in **MEMORY.md** for future reference
