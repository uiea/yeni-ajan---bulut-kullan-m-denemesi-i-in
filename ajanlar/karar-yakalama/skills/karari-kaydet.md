# Skill: Capture a Confirmed Decision

## Purpose
Record one confirmed, durable decision in the central register and in the relevant local context.

## When to Use This Skill
- The member confirms a project, customer, strategy, scope, priority, date, policy, or repeated workflow decision.
- The member says a proposal is final, approved, selected, or should be remembered.

## Do NOT Use This Skill For
- Ideas, questions, brainstorming, drafts, or routine work -> do not record them.
- Unclear or approval-gated items -> use `bekleyen-kararlari-gozden-gecir.md`.

## Serves Goal
- Preserve important decisions.
- Keep decisions discoverable.

## Inputs
- The exact confirmed decision.
- Why it was chosen, affected scope, owner, status, and relevant client/project when applicable.

## Process
1. Write a one-sentence decision summary and check that the member has clearly confirmed it.
2. Classify it as Workspace, Agent, Client, Project, Strategy, Process, or External action.
3. Add one row to `kararlar.md` with date, decision, reason, scope, owner, status, and local file links.
4. For a client or project decision, add a short decision note to its client skill and/or `brief.md` without duplicating unrelated detail.
5. Use `Onay bekliyor` when the decision needs a later external, destructive, or live action.
6. Report what was recorded and where.

## Output
- One central decision entry.
- A matching local note when the decision belongs to a client or project.

## Quality Bar
- Every entry answers what was decided, why, where it applies, who owns it, and what state it is in.
- The entry contains no secret or personal data.
- The decision is confirmed, not inferred.

## Rules & Feedback
- Record the decision, not the entire conversation.
  Why: a register must be searchable and durable.
  How to apply: use one concise sentence and a short rationale.
- A decision is not permission to perform a separate risky action.
  Why: strategy approval and execution approval are different.
  How to apply: retain `Onay bekliyor` until the member explicitly approves the specific action.
