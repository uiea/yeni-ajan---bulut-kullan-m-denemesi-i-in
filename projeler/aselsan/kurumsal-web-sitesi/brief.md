# Project Brief: ASELSAN Corporate Website Concept

## Project status and guardrails

- **Relationship:** ASELSAN is a target client, not a confirmed customer.
- **Output:** Original, local HTML/CSS concept for review only.
- **Representation:** Do not imply commission, endorsement, partnership, approval, or an official ASELSAN web presence.
- **Content:** Do not use official logos, proprietary imagery, confidential data, unverified metrics, or claims. Production copy and legal notices require rights-holder approval.

## Goal

Demonstrate a credible corporate-web direction that conveys advanced engineering, technological progress, reliability, and clarity, while giving a prospective stakeholder a concrete, responsive starting point for discussion.

## Primary audiences

1. Enterprise and public-sector decision makers evaluating a technology partner.
2. Engineering and technology stakeholders seeking capability-level information.
3. Prospective talent and collaborators who need a concise view of the organisation's direction.

## Proposed page architecture

| Section | User need | Concept content / interaction |
|---|---|---|
| Header | Navigate quickly | Concept navigation, compact mobile menu, prominent contact/discovery CTA. |
| Hero | Understand the proposition at a glance | Original technology-forward headline, short explanatory paragraph, primary and secondary CTA. |
| Focus areas | Scan capabilities | Three to six neutral capability cards; use placeholders until approved wording is available. |
| Innovation / progress | See evidence of forward motion | Timeline, signal-grid, or engineering-process visual with non-claiming copy. |
| Trust / operating principles | Assess credibility | Principles such as rigor, resilience, and responsible innovation; no unverified statistics. |
| Insights / careers preview | Find next paths | Optional teaser modules, clearly labelled as concept content. |
| Contact CTA and footer | Continue the conversation | Local-concept CTA, legal/disclaimer placeholder, accessibility and privacy links as placeholders. |

## Functional and technical scope

- Single-page, semantic HTML and responsive CSS.
- Desktop, tablet, and mobile layouts; keyboard-usable navigation and controls.
- Deep navy base, electric cyan/teal accents, white type, grid/orbital/signal motifs.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`; maintain legible contrast and visible focus states.
- Local static preview only. No analytics, forms that transmit data, third-party embeds, deployment, or external delivery.

## Out of scope

- Production CMS, authentication, multilingual implementation, real contact submission, analytics, SEO deployment, accessibility conformance certification, official brand implementation, and publication.

## Success measures for this concept

- A reviewer can identify the technology-and-progress positioning within the first screen.
- Key sections remain understandable and usable at mobile widths without horizontal scrolling.
- Primary navigation, CTA links, and any interactive element can be operated by keyboard with visible focus.
- The concept contains no claim that it is official or commissioned, and no unsupported factual/quantitative claims.
- Files are self-contained in this project folder and can be opened locally in a modern browser.

## Open questions before production work

1. What is the primary conversion: contact, partnership inquiry, recruitment, product discovery, or another action?
2. Which visitor group has priority, and what task must it complete?
3. Which pages, languages, content owners, and approval/legal workflow are required?
4. Are there approved brand assets, design-system rules, typography licenses, and image usage rights?
5. Which accessibility standard and browser/device support matrix apply?
6. Is a CMS, analytics, consent management, search, or authenticated area required?
7. What performance, security, privacy, hosting, and release requirements govern a real implementation?
